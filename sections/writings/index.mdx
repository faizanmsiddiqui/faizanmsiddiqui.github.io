# Writings

Welcome to my collection of writings! Here, you'll find a variety of pieces on a
wide range of topics.

I've organized the content into two main sections: [**Books**](/writings/books)
and [**Pages**](/writings/pages). Think of them like **Epics**[^epics] and
**Stories**[^stories] in **Agile Project Management**[^apm]:

- **Books** are long-form works focused on specific themes or subjects. They may
  look like traditional books, or they might be collections of articles meant
  for reference.
- **Pages** are shorter pieces like articles, blog posts, essays, opinions, or
  even short stories intended for quick reading and sharing.

The idea is to give you a clear distinction between longer, more comprehensive
works and shorter, more focused pieces. This makes it easier for you to find
what you need. While there's no strict rule for what goes where, Books tend to
be in a reference-style format, and Pages lean toward conversational or
narrative styles.

[^epics]:
    **Epics** are large bodies of work that can be broken down into smaller
    tasks or Stories. They represent significant features or initiatives in a
    project. Learn more:
    [**What is an Epic User Story? | Agile Alliance**](https://agilealliance.org/glossary/epic/)

[^stories]:
    **Stories** or **User Stories** are smaller, manageable units of work that
    describe a feature from an end-user perspective. Learn more:
    [**What are User Stories? | Agile Alliance**](https://agilealliance.org/glossary/user-stories/)

[^apm]:
    **Agile Project Management** is a project management methodology that
    emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and customer satisfaction. It often
    uses terms like Epics and Stories to organize work into manageable units.
    Learn more:
    [**What is Agile? | Agile 101 | Agile Alliance**](https://agilealliance.org/agile101/)
