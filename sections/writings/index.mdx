# Writings

Welcome to my collection of writings! Here, you will find a variety of pieces
covering a wide range of topics.

[**Books**](/writings/books) and [**Pages**](/writings/pages) are two distinct
sections I created to organize different types of content. They are similar to
Epics[^epics] and Stories[^stories] in Agile Project Management[^apm]. Books are
long-form works organized around specific themes or subjects. Pages are shorter
articles, blogs, essays, and opinions on diverse topics. Please don't confuse
these with traditional books and pages. Some of the Books here may resemble
actual books, while others are more like collections of articles for reference.
Similarly, Pages can be articles, blog posts, essays, or even short stories.

The Books section is primarily for my own reference, while the Pages section is
mainly intended for sharing with others. There is no strict rule for what goes
into each section, but the idea is to provide a clear distinction between
longer, more comprehensive works and shorter, more focused pieces. This allows
readers to easily navigate and find the type of content they are interested in.

[^epics]:
    **Epics** are large bodies of work that can be broken down into smaller
    tasks or Stories. They represent significant features or initiatives in a
    project. Further reading: https://agilealliance.org/glossary/epic/

[^stories]:
    **Stories** or **User Stories** are smaller, manageable units of work that
    describe a feature from an end-user perspective. Further reading:
    https://agilealliance.org/glossary/user-stories/

[^apm]:
    **Agile Project Management** is a project management methodology that
    emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and customer satisfaction. It often
    uses terms like Epics and Stories to organize work into manageable units.
    Further reading: https://agilealliance.org/agile101/
